Professional Development

  • Graduate studies with Dr. Bruce Stillman at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratories: Studied the mechanisms that regulate the initiation of DNA replication and identified Cdc45, a conserved factor required for initiation of DNA replication (Zou et al., MCB, 1997; Zou and Stillman, Science, 1998; Zou and Stillman, MCB, 2000).
  • Postdoctoral fellowship with Stephen Elledge at the Baylor College of Medicine: Published key work on understanding the DNA damage checkpoint and discovered that generation of single strand DNA was the molecular signal that triggered the activation of ATR (Zou and Elledge, Science, 2003; Zou et al., Genes Dev, 2001; Zou et al., PNAS, 2004).
  • Faculty position at Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School: Provided important insights into ATR signaling and the DNA damage response (Shiotani and Zou, Mol Cell, 2008; Liu et al., Mol Cell, 2011).

Current Research

  • ATR signalling in genome maintenance: Studied how ATR functions in chromosome segregation (Kabeche et al., Science, 2018).
  • Exploring ATR function in different aspects of genome maintenance: Investigated ATR's role in alternative lengthening of telomeres (Flynn et al., Nature, 2011; Flynn et al., Science, 2014; Zhang et al., Mol Cell, 2021; Yadav et al., Mol Cell, 2022) and protection of the genome from R-loops (Matos et al., Mol Cell, 2020).
  • Role of transcription in D-loop formation during homologous recombination: Examined the interplay between gene expression, DNA repair, and chromosome organization in D-loop formation (Ouyang et al., Nature, 2021).

Future Directions

  • Identifying types of replication stress associated with different cancer types: Research will focus on the DNA replication program, especially replication stress in cancer cells.
  • Targeting ATR and the DNA damage response for therapeutic potential in cancer cells: ATR inhibitors are being considered for specific cancer types, especially those reliant on ALT (Flynn et al., Nature, 2011; Flynn et al., Science, 2014; Zhang et al., Mol Cell, 2021; Yadav et al., Mol Cell, 2022).
  • Exploring ATR inhibitors in ALT+ leiomyosarcoma and osteosarcoma patients: Clinical trials are underway to assess the effect of ATR inhibitors (Nguyen et al., Cancer Res, 2018).
